# Institutional Friction

Institutional friction is the loss of coordination that appears when conversations lose context, meetings create ambiguous follow-ups, and people leave with different versions of what was decided.

## Why It Matters

The article argues that this is not primarily an agent problem. It is a human coordination problem that AI makes more visible because work accelerates while shared context remains fragile.

This friction causes a company to stop sharing reality: customer pain, unresolved commitments, product tradeoffs, and weak signals fail to propagate reliably.

## Typical Symptoms

- decisions exist, but their rationale is unclear
- commitments are created but not owned
- tickets and CRM fields compress away the original why
- dashboards stay clean while memory disappears
- teams act on conflicting assumptions
- leaders manage through summaries instead of contact with company truth

## Relation to Company Brain

A [[wiki/concepts/Company Brain]] is proposed as a response to institutional friction: it turns scattered communication and data into [[wiki/concepts/Organizational Memory]], [[wiki/concepts/Context Graph|context]], and [[wiki/concepts/Governed Action]].

The risk is that tools address only one layer: search handles facts, meeting notes handle transcripts, workflows handle actions, but friction persists if the relationships and rationale are missing. ^[inferred]
