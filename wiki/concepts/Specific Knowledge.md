# Specific Knowledge

Specific knowledge is knowledge that society cannot easily train someone else to perform. Naval Ravikant frames it as the opposite of interchangeable labor: if a school or company can train you, it can train a replacement.

## Key Ideas

- Specific knowledge is discovered by following genuine curiosity and passion rather than chasing whatever is currently fashionable.
- Building it feels like play to the person doing it, while looking like work from the outside.
- When it is taught, it tends to move through apprenticeship rather than formal schooling.
- It is often technical, creative, or both, which makes it harder to outsource or automate.
- Specific knowledge becomes economically powerful when paired with [[wiki/concepts/Accountability]] and [[wiki/concepts/Permissionless Leverage]].

## Why It Matters

Specific knowledge is a career-level version of [[wiki/topics/Learning Methodology]]: the goal is not merely to consume more information, but to build a personal structure of ability that is difficult for others to copy. ^[inferred]

This connects to [[wiki/topics/Mental Models]] because a rare skill is often carried by unusual models, taste, judgment, and pattern recognition, not only by explicit procedures. ^[inferred]

## Open Questions

- How can a person tell the difference between genuine curiosity and avoidance disguised as passion?
- Which parts of specific knowledge can be accelerated by AI without making the skill more replaceable?
