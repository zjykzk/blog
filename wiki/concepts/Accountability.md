# Accountability

Accountability is taking business risk under your own name. In Naval Ravikant's wealth model, society rewards visible responsibility with responsibility, equity, and leverage.

## Key Ideas

- Accountability is not only doing work; it is accepting name-attached consequences for judgment.
- The most accountable people often have singular, public, risky brands.
- Accountability makes [[wiki/concepts/Specific Knowledge]] legible: others can see who made the judgment and who should receive trust, blame, upside, or downside.
- Without accountability, leverage can magnify someone else's decisions while leaving you as rented time. ^[inferred]

## Risk And Reward

Accountability is uncomfortable because it exposes judgment to public evaluation. That exposure is also the routing mechanism that lets reputation, equity, and opportunity compound. ^[inferred]

This connects to [[wiki/topics/Technical Management]] and [[wiki/topics/Critical Thinking]]: judgment improves when decisions have consequences and feedback, not when the decision-maker can remain invisible. ^[inferred]
