# Permissionless Leverage

Permissionless leverage is leverage that does not require someone else to give you money or follow you. Naval Ravikant identifies code and media as the modern forms: they can work while you sleep and have near-zero marginal replication cost.

## Key Ideas

- Capital and labor are permissioned leverage: someone must fund you or follow you.
- Code and media are permissionless leverage: a person can create software, writing, audio, video, or other replicable artifacts without first receiving institutional permission.
- This makes the Internet a career-space expansion, not just a distribution channel.
- Permissionless leverage multiplies [[wiki/topics/Judgment Under Leverage]]; it does not replace judgment.
- If someone cannot code, Naval recommends books, blogs, videos, and podcasts as media leverage.

## Relation To Systems
