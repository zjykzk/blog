# Revised Bloom Taxonomy Source Guide

This page preserves the external learning-theory anchor from Anderson and Krathwohl's 2001 revision of Bloom's taxonomy.

## Source Claim

The Library of Congress record identifies the source as "A taxonomy for learning, teaching, and assessing: a revision of Bloom's taxonomy of educational objectives," edited by Lorin W. Anderson and David R. Krathwohl.

Carnegie Mellon's teaching guide describes the revised taxonomy as a framework of student-centered actions and notes that the updated version incorporates new knowledge and thought into Bloom's original framework.

## Use For Application Versus Creation

The revised taxonomy supports a formal separation between applying and creating. Application belongs to using knowledge in a given situation; creation belongs to generating, designing, constructing, or reorganizing elements into a coherent or functional whole.

This strengthens [[wiki/concepts/Knowledge Types]]: Bloom-like levels describe depth of handling, not the type of knowledge itself.

## Limitations

The taxonomy is a pedagogical classification, not a theory of all real-world creative work. In practice, creating often requires applying, analyzing, and evaluating at the same time. ^[inferred]
