# How to Get Rich Source Guide

This guide preserves the useful structure from Naval Ravikant's "How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" PDF. The PDF is image-backed, so text was extracted by local OCR from the provided document; minor OCR uncertainty remains around punctuation and formatting. ^[ambiguous]

## Source Claims

- Seek wealth, not money or status.
- Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep; money transfers time and wealth; status is position in a social hierarchy.
- Ethical wealth creation is possible.
- Renting out time will not create financial freedom; equity ownership is necessary.
- You get rich by giving society what it wants but does not yet know how to get, at scale.
- Play long-term games with long-term people.
- Returns in wealth, relationships, and knowledge come from compound interest.
- Pick partners with intelligence, energy, and integrity.
- Learn to sell and learn to build.
- Arm yourself with specific knowledge, accountability, and leverage.
- Specific knowledge comes from genuine curiosity and passion, is often technical or creative, and is taught through apprenticeship more than school.
- Accountability means taking business risks under your own name.
- Leverage comes from capital, labor, and products with no marginal cost of replication.
- Capital and labor are permissioned leverage; code and media are permissionless leverage.
- Leverage is a force multiplier for judgment.
- Foundational fields for judgment include microeconomics, game theory, psychology, persuasion, ethics, mathematics, and computers.
- Reading is faster than listening; doing is faster than watching.
- Work hard, but what you work on and who you work with matter more than raw effort.

## Distilled Pattern

The source is not mainly a finance note. It is a career-system model:

- [[wiki/topics/Wealth Creation]]: own scalable assets instead of only selling time.
- [[wiki/concepts/Specific Knowledge]]: build an ability that cannot be mass-trained.
- [[wiki/concepts/Accountability]]: attach your name to risk and judgment.
- [[wiki/concepts/Permissionless Leverage]]: use code and media when possible because they do not require approval from capital owners or followers.
- [[wiki/topics/Judgment Under Leverage]]: leverage magnifies decisions, so foundational judgment matters.

## Cautions

- The source is aphoristic and under-argued; it gives a compressed worldview, not empirical proof.
- The examples of public accountable brands are culturally specific and should not be treated as a universal moral ranking. ^[ambiguous]
- "You will get what you deserve" is motivationally useful but can understate structural inequality, luck, and path dependence. ^[inferred]
