# Schon Reflective Practice Source Guide

This page preserves the design-theory anchor from Donald Schön's reflection-in-action tradition.

## Source Claim

The Stanford interview chapter describes reflection in action as interacting with a model, encountering surprising results, making sense of them, and inventing new strategies of action from the new interpretation.

It also describes design as a conversation with materials, where experiments reveal implications that the designer did not fully anticipate.

## Use For Application Versus Creation

This source bridges [[wiki/concepts/Application]] and [[wiki/concepts/Creation]]. A designer may begin by applying a known model, but surprises in the situation can force reframing and invention.

This supports the distinction that application focuses on fit, while creation begins when fit requires a new frame, new move, or new order. ^[inferred]

## Limitations

The Stanford page is an interview/excerpt rather than the full *Reflective Practitioner* text. Use it as a practical access point, and cite the book for the deeper source.
