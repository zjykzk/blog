# How People Learn Transfer Source Guide

This page preserves the learning-science anchor from the National Research Council's *How People Learn*.

## Source Claim

The source says a major goal of schooling is preparing learners for flexible adaptation to new problems and settings.

It distinguishes memory-based performance from transfer: instructional differences become clearer when learners must extend learning to new problems and settings.

It also states that flexible use of knowledge depends on understanding when, where, why, and how to use knowledge, including the conditions of applicability.

## Use For Application Versus Creation

This source strengthens [[wiki/concepts/Application]] by grounding application in transfer, not repetition. Strong application means the learner can carry a principle across contexts while adapting it to local constraints.

It also explains why "照着做" is weak application: it may reproduce a practiced form without extracting the underlying features that make transfer possible. ^[inferred]

## Limitations

The source focuses on learning and education, so its claims should be adapted carefully when applied to design, management, or creative work outside school.
