# Csikszentmihalyi Systems Model of Creativity Source Guide

This page preserves the external research anchor from Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi's collected work on the systems model of creativity.

## Source Claim

The Springer overview presents the volume as a forty-year arc of creativity research, including problem solving versus problem finding, social context, creativity as social construction, and the systems model of creativity.

The table of contents includes "Society, Culture, and Person: A Systems View of Creativity," "Creativity and Genius: A Systems Perspective," and education-focused implications of the systems perspective.

## Use For Application Versus Creation

This source strengthens [[wiki/concepts/Creation]] by showing that creation is not only an inner act. A new form becomes creative through its relation to a domain, a social field, and a history of prior forms.

For the application-creation distinction, the useful point is: application can succeed privately when a method fits a situation; creation usually needs a new order that others can recognize, use, judge, or continue. ^[inferred]
