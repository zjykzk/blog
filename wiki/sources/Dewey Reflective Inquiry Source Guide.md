# Dewey Reflective Inquiry Source Guide

This page preserves the pragmatist anchor from John Dewey's *How We Think*, especially Chapter 6 on the complete act of thought.

## Source Claim

Dewey analyzes reflective thought as a process moving through felt difficulty, location and definition of the problem, possible solution, reasoning through implications, and observation or experiment that accepts or rejects the idea.

He also emphasizes that trained judgment adapts how much observation, idea formation, reasoning, and testing a situation requires.

## Use For Application Versus Creation

This source strengthens [[wiki/concepts/Application]] because it treats action under uncertainty as inquiry. Application is not merely executing a rule; it is fitting an idea to a problematic situation and testing consequences.

It also links to [[wiki/concepts/Creation]] when a possible solution must be transformed into a new form through experiment. ^[inferred]

## Limitations

Dewey is a broad philosophical and educational source. The mapping to application versus creation is a local synthesis rather than a direct Dewey taxonomy.
