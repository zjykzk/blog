# Amabile Componential Theory of Creativity Source Guide

This page preserves the external research anchor from Teresa M. Amabile's Harvard Business School working paper "Componential Theory of Creativity."

## Source Claim

Amabile defines creativity as producing a response, product, or solution that is both novel and appropriate to an open-ended task.

The theory says creative response depends on domain-relevant skills, creativity-relevant processes, intrinsic task motivation, and the surrounding social environment.

## Use For Application Versus Creation

This source strengthens [[wiki/concepts/Creation]] by preventing a weak equation between creation and novelty. Newness alone is not enough; creative output must fit a goal, task, or domain standard.

It also keeps creation connected to accumulated knowledge. Domain-relevant skill means creation usually reorganizes learned materials rather than appearing from nothing.

## Limitations

The source is a theory overview rather than a direct comparison of application and creation. Its relevance to [[wiki/syntheses/Application and Creation Distinction]] is partly inferential.
