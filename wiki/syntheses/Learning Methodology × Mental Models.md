# Learning Methodology × Mental Models

## The Connection

[[wiki/topics/Learning Methodology]] asks how to learn durably. [[wiki/topics/Mental Models]] asks what reusable thinking structures can be carried across situations.

Their connection is that durable learning is mental-model construction. The learner is not merely storing information; they are building compressed structures that can recognize situations, generate predictions, guide action, and transfer across contexts. ^[inferred]

## Where They Co-occur

The pair appears across the learning cluster:

- [[wiki/concepts/Understanding]] treats understanding as a movable concept cloud with examples, abstractions, and links.
- [[wiki/concepts/Analogy-Making]] explains transfer through structure mapping.
- [[wiki/concepts/AI Learning Tutor Loop]] uses AI to map fields, expose disagreements, diagnose understanding, and repair errors.
- [[wiki/sources/NotebookLM Learning Workflow Source Guide]] uses source-grounded prompts to build mental models before consuming details.
- [[wiki/sources/拥抱和桥接 知识迁移 Source Guide]] distinguishes near-context practice from abstract bridge-building.

## Cross-cutting Insight

A learning method should be evaluated by whether it changes the learner's model of the domain, not by whether it increases exposure to content. ^[inferred]

Summaries, highlights, and explanations are useful only if they become internal structure: categories, causal models, analogies, counterexamples, procedures, and judgment criteria. This makes AI-assisted learning risky when it short-circuits the learner's model-building work, and powerful when it supplies questions, contrast cases, disagreements, and feedback that force the model to reorganize. ^[inferred]

## Tensions and Trade-offs

- **Compression vs. distortion**: mental models simplify reality, but overcompression can hide exceptions and mechanisms.
- **AI assistance vs. learner agency**: AI can map a domain quickly, but the learner must still answer, fail, retrieve, apply, and revise.
- **Memorization vs. transfer**: facts matter, but durable use requires relations and conditions for applying them.
- **Model confidence vs. model testing**: a clear model can feel true before it survives examples, edge cases, and action feedback.
