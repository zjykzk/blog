# Human Agency × Narrative

## The Connection

[[wiki/concepts/Human Agency]] is the capacity to choose goals, call tools, create feedback, and take responsibility. [[wiki/concepts/Narrative]] is the structure that turns selected facts into a causal line for prediction, meaning, coordination, and action.

The connection is double-edged: agency needs narrative to move, but agency also requires the ability to inspect and replace narrative. ^[inferred]

## Where They Co-occur

The pair appears in the basic worldview and mechanism-model cluster:

- [[wiki/concepts/Hard Constraint]] checks narratives against resources, time, law, other people, and physical reality.
- [[wiki/concepts/Uncertainty as Meaning Fuel]] treats uncertainty as a condition for exploration and meaning, not only a defect to remove.
- [[wiki/concepts/三个自我模型]] separates momentary process, social interface, and deeper prediction/reward machinery.
- [[wiki/sources/能动与稳态生存逻辑 Source Guide]] contrasts old stability narratives with high-volatility agency.
- [[wiki/sources/Narrative as First Principle Source Guide]] frames narrative as an action interface.
- [[wiki/maps/Mechanism Models Map]] routes both concepts into mechanism-level thinking.

## Cross-cutting Insight

A person does not act on raw facts; they act on facts organized into a story about what matters, what game is being played, and what action makes sense. ^[inferred]

Agency therefore depends on narrative compression. But the same compression can become captivity: inherited family scripts, school scripts, career scripts, scarcity scripts, or status scripts can make a person feel active while they are only executing someone else's goal function. ^[inferred]

Mature agency is narrative authorship under constraint. It is not “believe any empowering story”; it is the ability to rewrite the story while letting reality veto it through feedback, hard constraints, and other people's agency. ^[inferred]

## Tensions and Trade-offs

- **Motivation vs. distortion**: strong narratives mobilize action, but can hide evidence and uncertainty.
- **Identity vs. adaptability**: stable stories help people persist, but old identities can block updating when the environment changes.
- **Meaning vs. constraints**: narrative gives hardship meaning, but can also romanticize ignoring resource, timing, or social limits.
- **Coordination vs. control**: shared stories enable group action, but can turn people into instruments of institutional or family scripts.

## Open Questions

- How can a person tell whether a motivating narrative is reality-refutable or self-sealing?
- What practices help people rewrite inherited narratives without losing continuity and responsibility?
- How should education preserve useful narratives while protecting agency from external control?
