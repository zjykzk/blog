# Wealth Creation

Wealth creation means owning assets that earn while you sleep. Naval Ravikant distinguishes wealth from money, which transfers time and wealth, and status, which marks position in a social hierarchy.

## Key Ideas

- Ethical wealth creation is possible; despising wealth makes it harder to build.
- Renting out time is not enough for financial freedom.
- Equity ownership is required because upside comes from owning part of a business or asset.
- Wealth comes from giving society something it wants but does not yet know how to get, at scale.
- Long-term games with long-term people matter because returns in wealth, relationships, and knowledge compound.
- Business partners should have intelligence, energy, and especially integrity.
- The source's compact formula is [[wiki/concepts/Specific Knowledge]] plus [[wiki/concepts/Accountability]] plus [[wiki/concepts/Permissionless Leverage]], governed by [[wiki/topics/Judgment Under Leverage]].

## Wealth, Money, And Status

The source makes a useful three-way split:

- **Wealth**: assets that keep producing value.
- **Money**: the transfer medium for time and wealth.
- **Status**: relative position in a hierarchy.

Status games can attack wealth-creation games because lowering someone else can raise relative standing. Wealth creation is positive-sum when it gives society a wanted product or service at scale. ^[inferred]

## Time, Equity, And Scale

The central economic constraint is that time does not scale. If income depends only on hours sold, upside remains capped. Equity, code, media, capital, and organizations change the scaling structure. ^[inferred]

This links directly to [[wiki/concepts/Leverage Points]]: a career can be redesigned around assets and replication instead of effort alone. ^[inferred]

## Open Questions

- How should "what society wants but does not yet know how to get" be tested without drifting into trend chasing?
- Where is the boundary between ethical wealth creation and extraction?
