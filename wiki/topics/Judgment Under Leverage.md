# Judgment Under Leverage

Judgment under leverage is the problem of making decisions whose effects are multiplied. Naval Ravikant states that leverage is a force multiplier for judgment: it magnifies the consequences of decisions.

## Key Ideas

- Judgment means making decisions.
- Leverage magnifies the consequences of those decisions.
- Judgment requires experience, but can be built faster by studying foundational fields.
- Naval rejects "business" as a single skill and points instead to microeconomics, game theory, psychology, persuasion, ethics, mathematics, and computers.
- Reading is faster than listening; doing is faster than watching.

## Why It Matters

Leverage without judgment is just amplified error. This makes [[wiki/topics/Critical Thinking]], [[wiki/topics/Mental Models]], and [[wiki/topics/Probability]] practical economic infrastructure, not abstract self-improvement topics. ^[inferred]

The source's advice also fits [[wiki/topics/Thinking in Systems]]: a leveraged actor should study fields that reveal incentives, feedback, human behavior, ethics, formal reasoning, and computation. ^[inferred]
